Thành phố Hồ Chí Minh

Fri 27 Nov 2016

Ho Chi Minh City is the largest city in Vietnam and is located near the Mekong River. It is a popular tourist destination known for its French colonial architecture, including Notre-Dame Basilica, made entirely of materials imported from France, and the neoclassical Saigon Central Post Office. Food stalls line the streets of the city, especially around bustling Ben Thanh Market.

Thành phố Đà Nẵng

Sat 28 Nov 2016

Da Nang city is a coastal city in central Vietnam known for its sandy beaches and history as a French colonial port. It is a popular base for visiting the inland Bà Nà hills to the west of the city. Here the hillside Hải Vân Pass has views of Da Nang Bay and the Marble Mountains, five limestone outcrops that are home to pagodas and caves containing Buddhist shrines.

Thành phố Hà Nội

Sun 29 Nov 2016

Hanoi, the capital of Vietnam, is known for its centuries-old architecture and a rich culture with Southeast Asian, Chinese and French influences. At its heart is the chaotic Old Quarter, where the narrow streets are roughly arranged by trade. There are many little temples, including Bach Ma, honoring a legendary horse, plus Đồng Xuân Market, selling household goods and street food.
